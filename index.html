About

I am a PhD researcher in Medical Physics at the University of Rome Tor Vergata, Italy, with a specialization in visual brain decoding and connectomics. My research is inherently interdisciplinary, combining neuroimaging, computational modeling, and data-driven analysis to investigate the functional and structural organization of the human brain.

I hold a Master's degree in Biomedical Engineering from the University of Airlangga, Indonesia, where my thesis “Hypertension Classification Using PPG Signals: A Deep Learning Approach” demonstrated expertise in advanced deep learning techniques for medical signal processing. Prior to that, I earned my Bachelor's degree in Computer Systems Engineering from the Islamia University of Bahawalpur, Pakistan.

My current research interests include:

  • Brain Decoding & Visual Stimulus Reconstruction
  • Functional & Structural Connectomics
  • Graph Neural Networks & Graph Structural Learning
  • Neuroimaging (fMRI) Analysis
  • Deep Learning for Medical Signal & Image Processing
  • Computational Neuroscience
